🔒 How is this safe?

🔑 Your passphrase never leaves your device as-is. It is converted into an encryption key using SHA-256 hashing — the original words are never stored on any server.

📦 Your backup is encrypted before upload. Your identity and group keys are encrypted with AES-256-GCM (military-grade) using your passphrase-derived key + a unique random salt. The encrypted blob is then stored on Firebase.

🔍 Firebase sees only gibberish. The server stores your backup at a hashed path — it cannot determine whose backup it is, what's inside, or reverse the hash to find your passphrase.

🛡 Even identical passphrases are safe. Each backup uses a unique random salt, so two users with the same passphrase produce completely different encryption keys and ciphertext.

Your Data

🔒

End-to-End Encrypted

Your expenses are encrypted before they leave your device. No one can read your data.

How it works
🔑
Unique key per group
A random AES-256 encryption key is generated on your device. Same encryption used by banks.
🔗
Key in the invite link
The key is in the # part of the URL — never sent to any server. Only people you share the link with can read the data.
📦
Server sees only gibberish
Everything is encrypted into unreadable blobs before syncing. The server is a blind mailbox.
📱
Your device is the source
All data is stored locally. The server is just a sync pipe — if it disappears, your data stays.

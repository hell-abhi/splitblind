Your Data

🔒

End-to-End Encrypted

Your expenses are encrypted on your device before they leave it. No one — not even us — can read your data.

How it works
🔑 A unique key is created per group
When you create a group, a random AES-256 encryption key is generated on your device. This is the same encryption standard used by banks and governments.
🔗 The key lives in the invite link
The key is embedded in the # part of the invite URL. This part is never sent to any server — it stays in your browser. Only people you share the link with can decrypt the group's data.
📦 Firebase stores only encrypted blobs
Your expenses are encrypted into unreadable gibberish before being sent for syncing. The sync server acts as a blind mailbox — it delivers data but cannot read it.
📱 Your device holds the truth
All your data is stored locally on your device. The server is just a sync pipe. Even if it disappears tomorrow, your local data remains intact.

✅ The bottom line
Even if someone has the source code AND full access to the server, they cannot read your data without your group's invite link. The encryption key never touches any server.
